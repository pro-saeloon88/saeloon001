Affiliations

Saeloon had links to two distinct online groups. Accounts are based on participant recollections:

  • SIMP / WHYTBS: former independent chat associated with some Saeloon founders.
  • Salon Fawzi Cultural: informal cultural circle overlapping with Saeloon.

Background

Saeloon was founded in 2020 by Mohamed Khaled and a small circle of friends. It started as a casual social space for memes, conversation, and daily life topics.

Name & Identity

In 2021 Mohamed Khaled proposed a stable group identity: "Saeloon". This gave continuity across recurring group themes and inside jokes.

Symbolism & Flag

Mohamed Khaled designed a black-and-white skull flag inspired by pirate iconography. The flag was used as an internal emblem.

Organization & Membership

No formal governance or territory existed. Leadership was informal, based on participation and social influence. Membership fluctuated, with a core group recognized as founders.

Incidents

Ahmalid: Youngest member left after conflict.

Mazen: Security and interpersonal dispute during 2020–2022.

Influences & Internal Change

Members cited internet culture, alternative media aesthetics, and Jack Stauber’s work as influences. Changes in personal life affected group dynamics.

Dissolution

On 5 November 2024, Mohamed Khaled closed the public group. Final message:

"@Everyone, this will be enough, comrades, and to the great people of Saeloon. We must leave now, but Saeloon will remain in our hearts forever. I entrust you to God, whose trust is never lost."

Legacy

The Saeloon identity remains an archival and mnemonic artifact of a small online community rather than an ongoing public movement.
